Generating ideas for viral games is a mix of structured creativity, research, and iteration. Here’s a systematic approach that actually works in the mobile/indie space:

1️⃣ Start With Proven Mechanics
Look at existing viral hits and their core loops.
Identify simple mechanics with addictive potential:
Swipe puzzles (Candy Crush, 2048)
Word games (Wordle, Wordscapes)
Physics challenges (Angry Birds)
Incremental / idle loops (Idle Miner, Egg, Inc.)
Don’t copy—recombine mechanics in new ways.

💡 Tip: Make a list of 20+ mechanics and map how they could mix.

2️⃣ Combine Contrasting Genres
Viral games often blend familiar mechanics with surprising twists.
Examples:
Box-pushing + word puzzle (your earlier idea)
Match-3 + RPG leveling
Endless runner + tower defense
The novelty comes from unexpected synergy.
3️⃣ Observe Trends & Social Hooks
Check trending games on:
App Store / Google Play top charts
Steam trending / New & Noteworthy
TikTok / YouTube gaming clips
Identify themes, art styles, or mechanics that are gaining traction.

💡 Bonus: Look for micro-trends (daily challenges, AI puzzles, hyper-casual twists).

4️⃣ Leverage Simple Constraints
Constraints force creativity:
Single-button control
Limited grid size
One color palette
Many viral games are mechanically simple but cleverly constrained, e.g., Flappy Bird, Threes!.
5️⃣ Brainstorm With a “Hook Filter”

For each idea, ask:

Can a new player understand it in 10 sec?
Does it produce “aha” moments or satisfying loops?
Can players share it easily (score, achievement, funny moment)?
Ideas that pass all 3 → keep.
Ideas that fail → discard or iterate.
6️⃣ Use Idea Generation Frameworks
SCAMPER: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse.
Random Pairing: Pick 2 unrelated things → fuse mechanics.
Example: “sliding blocks” + “music” = rhythm-puzzle slider game
Analogy: Look at non-gaming behaviors (stacking plates, cooking, juggling letters) → gamify them.
7️⃣ Prototype Fast & Test
Viral ideas live or die by instant playability.
Prototype with paper, Figma, or simple code.
Show it to 5–10 friends or target users → watch reactions.
Iterate until “people can’t stop trying it once they get it”.
8️⃣ Add the Virality Factor
Shareable visuals: funny outcomes, memes, badges
Short clips for social media
Leaderboards or challenges
Daily rewards that create habitual loops

💡 Viral games often combine mechanical simplicity + social shareability + small surprise moments.
